A Compass to Get Lost

When you only care about where you want to end up and not how to get there. The arrow will always point to your destination so you can make your own route.

If you need the time-optimized route, use a maps app. But you might not want the fastest route when you are exploring a city. Use the arrow as a guide to create a route optimized for what is interesting around you.

The idea behind this compass is that we should adapt the amount of information that we use, based on what we need at the moment. It is useful to have more information, but it can be distracting or confusing when there is too much.

I wanted to make a physical device, with a real arrow, and that looks like an old compass. In practice this is too big to be that useful. It will be fun to use the idea in a more portable form factor in a future project.

How it's made: Arduino with a magnetometer, GPS, motor, lcd screen, potentiometer button and battery. I hand machined the brass parts and 3D printed the chassis.

Road Tripping

Have you ever returned to where you used to live or vacationed, just to drive around those areas? I am trying to capture the nostalgia of driving along familiar roads to see what has changed.

I made a script that takes a Google Maps route and creates a video as if you were driving along those roads. The video is every Streetview image along the route. You can choose to look out the front of the car as if you are driving, or out the side window as a passenger.

Last year, I drove with my family to where we used to spend our summers. It was so fun to sit in the back seat of the car and stare out the window. I was inspired to share the experience of nostalgic road trips, driving along old commutes.

I would love to turn this into something more immersive like putting screens behind car windows. I want it to be a shared experience with family and friends, all sitting together in a real car.

How it's made: the python script first maps the route using the Google Direction API. It then downloads images at every interpolated GPS coordinate using the Streetview API. It downloads multiple viewing angles at each point, and uses openCV to merge them into one. Then it creates the video out of the images.

You can see the code in github.

My First Aid Kit

This first aid kit is inspired by Tom Sachs's Bloody Finger Repair Station. I added a Days Since Last Injury Counter inside. Injuries are less painful when you get to push a reset button.

How it's made: plywood box in the correct colors. The counter is a real time clock, button, 7 segment display, and Arduino on perf board.

Pickle Puck

The Pickle Puck is a wireless sensor that tracks the progress of food fermentation. It monitors what is going on inside the fermentation barrel for better food safety, and consistent, delicious results.

The pH, temperature and dissolved solids sensors go in the fermentation barrel. The data collected creates a fermentation profile for each recipe. These profiles can adapt to temperature, ripeness, water content or other ingredient changes. The profiles can predict when the food is perfect to jar, and identify any issues along the way.

When I was working as a cook, I would try to get better at fermenting foods by reading books and papers on fermentation, and talking to fermented food companies. I found a paper that linked flavor, time, temperature, pH, and salt in kimchi fermentation curves. I was also learning about how commercial food fermentation processes control variables. I set out to make a device that tracks the important progress indicators to adapt to variations and improve outcomes.

Effect of Temperature and Salt Concentration on Kimchi Fermentation (Mheen and Kwon, 1984)

I made a prototype, got accepted into a hardware incubator, and found a couple companies willing to test it.

How it's made: Arduino with pH, temperature and dissolved solids sensors. 3D printed chassis in a waterproof container. Not pictured is the main battery.

Two New Devices: Winnow Hardware

Full redesign of two hardware devices to merge internal components onto new PCBs, replace the android tablets, and lower the Bill of Material cost by 24% with 42% fewer parts. Plus it looks great.

Winnow is working to reduce food waste in commercial kitchens. The hardware devices identify the all of the wasted food using computer vision or user input. The chef receives a list of the most wasted items and they can make or buy less of those to save food and food cost.

I led the hardware team to redesign two hardware devices. The larger device has a camera and a GPU to identify the food waste using computer vision on the edge. The smaller device does not have a camera and relies on user input.

YouTube AdBlock

This can identify in-video YouTube advertisements and skip over them. But do we have a responsibility to watch ads?

This analyzes a YouTube video transcript using large language models to identify in video advertisements. Once it identifies the parts of the video that are ads it creates an embedded video that skips ahead to timestamps past the ads. It could also identify and skip introductions, outros, or other parts of the video that you’d like to skip.

I am starting with YouTube videos because the website provides automatically generated transcripts. This method of skipping ads could also work with podcasts by using a transcription model before the large language model.


I love watching YouTube videos, and I appreciate that the creators are paid by advertisement. When I watch a video do I have a responsibility to sit through the ads?

If an ad comes on when I watch TV, I change channels. When I listen to podcasts, I mash the skip forward button to skip ads. When I watch youtube videos I hit the arrow keys to jump ahead. I have banner blindness to ads that I see on the internet. If I already am not listening to or watching the ads, is it cheating to skip the ads using automation?
